- Reliability regimes: `clean`, `degraded`
- Budget tiers: `loose`, `tight`

**Primary metric**:


## Latest run: semantic visual producer

## What I expected vs what I see

Expected after replacing `cv2_stats` with frozen semantic visual features:

- non-text templates, especially `TV`, `AV`, or `TAV`, should become meaningfully more useful in degraded slices
- `best_single_axis_oracle_regret_dt` should move toward or above 3 macro-F1
- clean-loose vs degraded-tight ranking should invert more strongly

Observed:

- semantic visual features improved the primary regret from 0.740 to 1.869
- the result is still below the 3 macro-F1 positive threshold
- Kendall tau-b became worse/high at 0.905 and rank inversion is only 0.048
- `T` remains the dominant template in clean-loose and degraded-tight
- `joint` reaches feasible oracle, but the oracle itself is only 1.869 above the best single-axis policy

Current classification:

```text
negative-but-diagnostic
```

This is not a positive AutoFusion-Bench result. It suggests semantic vision helped but did not make MELD a strong primary benchmark-signal dataset under the current degradation design.

## Hypotheses for the gap

H1: MELD is text-dominant under this feature-level setup. `T` alone remains strong enough that the best single-axis policy is close to feasible oracle.

H2: Current degradation is not creating enough modality-specific rank inversion. The degraded cell may lower scores without forcing meaningful template switching.

H3: Semantic visual features are useful but the simple sklearn template heads and convergence warnings may suppress the visual contribution. However, tuning heads too much could turn the pilot into architecture search.

H4: The budget axis is operationally valid enough to run the protocol, but the semvis run's preferred `TAV`-vs-unimodal cost separation warning may weaken the hard-budget story.

H5: The primary metric is strict, but relaxing it now would risk turning a diagnostic negative into an overclaimed positive. A triage label may be appropriate, but the paper-level threshold should probably remain.
